  See `scripts/sif/README.md`. **CPU-only** — the course has **no GPU access** (confirmed); every step
  below is chosen to run on CPU (and to parallelise across cores where it matters). Cellpose (**v3**,
  not v4), Baysor, and Proseg are installed for L1 segmentation; **CellMapper (fast-CCA)** + CellTypist
  for L2 annotation (Harmony is installed but not used — benchmarked ~8× slower for no gain; see L2 §2).

- **Notebooks** `[confirm]`**:** per level, `analysis/levelN/<NN>_<slug>_student.ipynb` +
  executed `..._solution.ipynb` (mirrors the `cajal_comp_proj` template). Student = guidance, no
  solutions; solution = fully executed.
- **Outputs:** processed artifacts → repo `data/`; figures → repo `figures/`. Name per level below.
- **Withholding:** student notebooks must **not** name the paper, cite its figures, or reveal cell
  types/niches before the **L2 reveal**. Say "vendor / legacy watershed baseline", not "the paper".
- **Ground-truth comparison (L2):** students *work* from `wang2025_merfish_cells_student.h5ad`
  (answer columns stripped; generated by [`data_download/make_student_data.py`](file:///shared/projects/tp_2630_ubordeaux_neuromics_184418/projects/C15/data_download/make_student_data.py) — run once)

  *(The earlier strict "never load the original in a student notebook" rule is relaxed: the executed

## Biological story — instructor background (the narrative behind L2)

> Distilled from the paper's full text (Zotero `KL76TR34`). This is the biology students should
> *recover and recognise* in L2 — the spine of the notebooks is "did we see this?", not metrics.
> Names the paper freely (instructor doc); student-facing reveal happens at L2.

**The big picture.** A paired snMultiome (RNA+ATAC) atlas of the developing human neocortex, first
trimester → adolescence, in two areas (**PFC** and primary visual cortex **V1**), with **MERFISH**
spatial transcriptomics on a 6-sample subset (2nd trimester → infancy). Two messages: (1) cell-type-,
age- and area-specific **gene-regulatory networks** build the cortex radially; (2) the
**neurogenesis→gliogenesis transition** runs through a **tripotential intermediate progenitor
(Tri-IPC, formerly "IPC-glia")** that locally makes GABAergic interneurons, OPCs and astrocytes —
and which GBM tumours transcriptionally mimic. GWAS integration puts ASD risk on 2nd-trimester
IT excitatory neurons.

**Taxonomy (5 classes / 11 subclasses / 33 types; 29 detectable in the 300-gene MERFISH panel).**
Classes = progenitor / neuron / glia / immune / vascular. Lineages students should know by sight:
- **Radial glia (progenitors):** RG-vRG (ventricular), RG-tRG (truncated), RG-oRG (outer). Markers
  TFAP2C; CRYAB specific to tRG.
- **Excitatory (EN-\*):** IPC-EN (progenitor; EOMES/TBR2) → EN-Newborn → EN-IT-Immature →
  layer-specific IT (EN-L2_3/L4/L5/L6-IT) + non-IT (EN-L5-ET, EN-L5_6-NP, EN-L6-CT, EN-L6b). Markers
  TBR1, SATB2, CUX2 (upper IT), RORB (L4), FEZF2 (deep/non-IT).
- **Inhibitory (IN-\*), by origin:** MGE → IN-MGE-SST (SST/CORT), IN-MGE-PV (PVALB); CGE → IN-CGE-VIP,
  -SNCG, IN-mix-LAMP5; **IN-dLGE-immature → renamed IN-NCx_dGE-immature** (MEIS2/SP8/TSHZ1/PBX3).
- **Macroglia:** **IPC-Glia → Tri-IPC** (EGFR-high, OLIG2+), Astrocytes (immature/protoplasmic/fibrous;
  GFAP/AQP4), OPC (PDGFRA/OLIG2), Oligodendrocytes (SOX10/MYRF).
- **Other:** Cajal–Retzius (layer 1), Microglia (IRF8), Vascular.

**Radial architecture — what the niches recover (ventricle → pia).** **VZ/SVZ** (progenitors: RG,
IPC-EN, Tri-IPC) → **intermediate zone** (migration) → **cortical plate** laminated **inside-out**
(deep L6/subplate → L5 → L4 → L2/3 → L1; non-IT born before IT). **Layer 1** = Cajal–Retzius.
**White matter + meninge** = oligodendrocytes + IN-NCx_dGE-immature (future white-matter interstitial
INs). Interneurons migrate via **two routes** (marginal zone vs VZ/SVZ).

**Figure 1 (multiome reference, *light* in L2):** 1b UMAP of the 33 types by lineage; 1c age/region;
1d cell-type proportions across stages (progenitors deplete; upper-layer IT + macroglia rise after
birth; EN-L4-IT enriched in V1); 1e epigenome–transcriptome concordance (PAX6/EMX2 in RG).

**Figure 2 — the MERFISH figure L2 reproduces (panel by panel):**
- **2a** — six samples in physical space, each cell coloured by **type** and by **niche**. **10 niches**
  = k-means on each cell's **50 nearest spatial neighbours**, named for cortical domains: *layer 1 ·
  upper layer in development · layer 2/3 · layer 4 · layer 5 · layer 6 & subplate · intermediate zone
  · white matter & meninge · VZ & SVZ · dorsal LGE*.
- **2b** — niche composition (cell-type proportion per niche): progenitors in VZ/SVZ; mature ENs in
  their layers; OPCs grey+white, oligodendrocytes mostly white matter; IN-NCx_dGE-immature in white
  matter across all ages.
- **2c** — **neighbourhood enrichment** (`squidpy.gr.nhood_enrichment`, PFC-infancy sample): ENs enriched
  in their own layer; cross-class hits **EN-IT-immature↔IN-CGE-VIP** and **EN-L4-IT↔IN-MGE-SST**.
- **2d** — NCEM intercellular communication: strong EN↔EN, EN↔IN; **EN-IT-immature** and **IN-MGE-SST**
  as key senders.
- **2e** — **neuregulin** signalling **from EN-IT-immature** (NRG1/2/3 → ERBB3/ERBB4).
- **2f** — **somatostatin** signalling **from IN-MGE-SST** (SST/CORT → SSTR1/SSTR2); slice-culture
  agonist experiment shows SST regulates EN maturation. *(L2 communication target: recover 2e/2f hits
  with LIANA / `squidpy.gr.ligrec`.)*

**Headline findings (context; mostly L3 territory):** Tri-IPC tripotency and the GW18–26
neurogenesis→gliogenesis switch (Fig 4); GBM mimics Tri-IPCs (Fig 4h–j); SCENIC+ GRNs / 582 eRegulons
(Fig 3); GWAS — ASD↔EN-IT-immature, RG↔executive function, microglia↔working memory (Fig 5).

**Open questions (ready-made L3 anchors):** fate of perinatal **EN-Newborn** (grey/white/olfactory-bulb?);
whether **IN-NCx_dGE-immature** become white-matter interneurons; mechanism of the surprising GWAS
associations. *(Caveat: the exact 300-gene panel = Suppl. Table 5b and the marker→type map =
Extended Data Fig. 4b are referenced but not in the extracted text — confirm gene lists against the
supplement if a definitive panel is needed.)*

## Level 1 — From raw spatial output to clean cells · 1 day · *Sopa-powered* · (Sample A only)

*"What did the instrument measure, and which cells can we trust?"*

1. **Load** `UCSF2018-003-MFG_baseline.zarr` (SpatialData; contents in §Staged data). z3-only, 90,962 cells.
2. **Explore & QC** — transcripts/cell, genes/cell, in-situ markers over DAPI/polyT; control
   probes, volume, counts, doublets. (Standard MERSCOPE QC — keep student framing neutral.)
3. **Segmentation comparison (core)** — four strategies via **one Sopa chain**
   (`make_transcript_patches` → `segmentation.{cellpose,baysor,proseg}` → `aggregate`; exact
   params per the Sopa tutorials in L0). Transcript methods share a fixed Cellpose prior, isolating one variable:

   | # | Strategy | Notes |
   |---|---|---|
   | 1 | **Author** | the watershed `authors_cells` shapes (baseline) |
   | 2 | **Cellpose** | DAPI nuclei ± polyT cyto; in env |
   | 3 | **Cellpose + Baysor** | Baysor refines the Cellpose prior; binary at `…/C15/software/bin/baysor` |
   | 4 | **Cellpose + Proseg** | Proseg refines the *same* prior; `rust-proseg` (bioconda) |

   The shared Cellpose prior + whole-sample Proseg are set on **`make_transcript_patches`**
   (`prior_shapes_key="cellpose_boundaries"`, `patch_width=None`), *not* on the segmentation calls.
   Tests: 1→2 watershed vs Cellpose; 2→{3,4} does transcript refinement help; **3 vs 4** Baysor vs Proseg.
4. **Quantify quality.** Harmonize first (Author + Proseg have counts; Cellpose & Cellpose+Baysor
   need `sopa.aggregate`). **Salas-style scatter**, one point per method, best = upper-right
   (Salas et al. / [txsim](https://github.com/theislab/txsim); [ResolVI](https://github.com/YosefLab/resolvi-reproducibility) `_utils.py`):
   - **x = mean transcripts per cell** (report median too). *Not % assigned* — % assigned and purity
     are *both* trivially maxed by over-splitting into 1-transcript cells; transcripts/cell penalizes
     that, so the axes guard each other (purity catches over-merging, transcripts/cell catches
     over-splitting). Compute consistently across methods (same QC).
   - **y = negative-marker purity (label-free, gene-pair level):** from the reference
     (`wang2025_multiome.h5mu` RNA, `class` lineages — neuron/glia/vascular/immune; **skip
     progenitors**, developmentally non-exclusive), take lineage-specific panel-gene markers and form
     **cross-lineage gene pairs** (≈0 co-expression in reference single cells). Call each gene "on"
     per spatial cell with a **per-gene 2-component mixture** (signal vs background), *not* `count>0`.
     Per pair, double-positive rate = `P(both on | either on)`; **purity = 1 − mean over pairs**.
     *(Lineage labels only pick the cross-lineage pairs — no expression pooling; block-average pairs
     by lineage for the optional ResolVI Fig-2E-style matrix.)*

   Label-free on the spatial side → works pre-annotation. Reimplement ~30–40 lines (both source repos
   unlicensed) and **cite Salas et al. + ResolVI** (we *reimplement* the ~30–40-line metric on CPU —
   ResolVI itself is **not** run in L1, so no scvi-tools dependency); document the marker-selection threshold + mixture
   recipe for student/solution parity. *(Stretch: `prior_shapes_key="auto"` refines the vendor prior — "does the prior matter?")*

**Deliverable:** a metric-backed segmentation choice on Sample A. **Self-contained — its output is
*not* carried into L2** (re-segmenting only one sample would batch-confound the cohort).

## Level 2 — Cells → tissue architecture · 1 day · *paper reveal · reproduce Fig 2* · (all 6 samples)

*"What cell types are here, how are they organised, how do they communicate?"*

1. **Load the cohort** — `wang2025_merfish_cells_student.h5ad` (🔒 never the unstripped file).
   Normalize; light cross-sample integration. *Start fresh from author segmentation — do not reuse L1.*
2. **Reference mapping / annotation** — reference = the RNA modality (staged as
   `wang2025_multiome_rna.h5ad`, gene-filtered; transfer the **`type`** column → target the 29
   MERFISH-detectable types). Taught path: **CellMapper** (CPU) — build a shared latent space with
   CellMapper's own **fast-CCA** joint embedding (`compute_fast_cca`), then transfer labels **and impute
   the transcriptome** over a `pynndescent` kNN. *(We benchmarked Harmony as the joint space and it was
   ~8× slower for no accuracy gain, so fast-CCA is the taught path; the notebook still shows the
   cross-sample batch structure. Use `only_yx=True` — CellMapper otherwise builds 4 kNN graphs when only
   query→reference is needed.)* **CellTypist** as an external
   pretrained-reference baseline: `Developing_Human_Brain` (first-trimester — note the stage gap vs our
   2nd-trimester→infancy) and `Adult_Human_PrefrontalCortex` (matches the PFC samples). *(Dropped:
   DiagVI / scANVI — GPU-favoured scvi-tools; scanpy `ingest` ≈ CellMapper with a weaker latent space.)*
   Validate: (i) canonical markers; (ii) **held-out-gene correlation** — withhold a fixed seeded subset
   of panel genes (CellMapper masks them via `mask_var="is_train"`, so they never enter the latent
   space), impute, correlate per gene. **⚠️ Library-size correction:** pass CellMapper's `target_libsize`
   (per-cell sum over the *training* genes) to `map_layers` so imputed counts match each spatial cell's
   sequencing depth — without it the held-out correlations are meaningless.

4. **Cell–cell communication** — paper used NCEM + CellChat; students reproduce the **concept** with

- **snATAC / multiome integration** — GRNs, peak-to-gene, motif activity (`wang2025_multiome.h5mu`
  `ATAC`: 82,505 peaks, coords in `var`; `X_wnn.umap` computed). Authors' `SCENICplus/` is the reference. *(Strong.)*
- **Tri-IPC / interneuron spatial mapping** — locate Tri-IPCs (IPC-glia) + progeny
  **IN-NCx_dGE-immature** (renamed from IN-dLGE-immature; MEIS2+/SP8+) in white matter across stages
  (Ext. Fig. 5); test the neurogenesis→gliogenesis gradient. *(Strong — anchors below.)*

  — opt-in, CPU-slow.)*
- **Disease-risk mapping** — GWAS risk onto spatial types/niches. *(Trickier; scope carefully.)*

**The paper's own open questions (ready-made anchors):**
